📜 Terms of Service Agreement

By scheduling an appointment, paying a deposit, or receiving services from Round The Clock Notary, you agree to the following terms and conditions in full.

Service Description

Round The Clock Notary provides mobile notary and remote online notarization services in accordance with Texas law. The notary does not represent any party and does not participate in the legal content of documents. The State of Texas sets the maximum fee for each notarial act. Travel, scheduling, printing, and convenience services are separate fees.

Legal Advice Disclaimer

The notary public is not an attorney and does not provide legal advice. If legal advice is required, consult a licensed attorney before scheduling.

Identification Requirements

All signers must present valid government-issued photo ID. The name on the ID must match the document being notarized.

Deposit & Appointment Policy

A deposit is required to secure all appointments and is applied toward the final total. The deposit is non-refundable if: cancellation is less than 24 hours before the appointment; signer fails to appear; no call/no show after the 15-minute wait; appointment cannot be completed due to missing ID or incomplete documents.

Travel Fees

Travel fees apply once the notary begins travel and are non-refundable even if the notarization cannot be completed.

Right to Refuse Service

The notary must refuse service if proper ID is not presented, the signer appears unwilling or unaware, the document is incomplete, or fraud is suspected. The deposit is not refunded in these cases.

Limitation of Liability

Liability is limited to the lesser of total fees paid or the notary's E&O insurance limits. Round The Clock Notary is not liable for indirect, consequential, or punitive damages.

Indemnification

Client agrees to indemnify and hold harmless Round The Clock Notary from any claims arising from the legal content or use of documents presented for notarization.

Governing Law

This agreement is governed by Texas law. Disputes shall be mediated in Hood County, Texas before any legal action.

No Call / No Show

If the notary is not contacted within 15 minutes of the scheduled appointment time, the appointment is considered a no-show and the deposit is forfeited.
